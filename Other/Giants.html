Giant

Giants are giants in ancient Greek mythology.In Hesiod, the giants are deities, the sons of Gaia, who was fertilized by drops of blood that fell from the genitals of Uranus cut off by Kronos, as well as by the seed of her second husband, the cruel and terrible deity Tartarus. According to Homer, wild, gigantic and related to the gods creatures; their king Eurymedon was killed by the gods, along with all his people, for impiety.

Giants compare favorably with their gnome relatives not only in height, but also in the strength of their blows, as well as in their abilities that allow them to crush entire armies.
