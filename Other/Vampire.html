Vampire

A vampire or ghoul, or ghoul, is a character in the lower mythology of the peoples of Europe; most often a half-dead person, or undead, or less often, an animal that leads a nocturnal lifestyle, sucks blood from people and gives nightmares. According to the mythological ideas of the Slavs, the so-called vampires became vampires.

“Hostage dead” are criminals, suicides, who died a premature death or were infected from the bites of other vampires. In folklore, the term is usually used to refer to a blood-sucking creature from Eastern European legends, but vampires are often used to refer to similar characters in the myths and legends of other peoples.
