Armor

A doublet is a thick, fitted jacket with a quilted linen or silk lining that reaches mid-thigh. A thick stand-up collar protects the throat. A doublet is more clothing than armor.

A mail shirt is a lightweight piece of chain mail sandwiched between two layers of fabric or leather that covers the torso and shoulders, the ideal compromise between weight, protection and freedom of movement.

Plate is armor made of large metal plates that covers the entire body - from head to toe. Due to its heaviness, it is used primarily by riders. The armor is legendary strong, but expensive, and is made to order. A warrior in armor from someone else's shoulder will look very unpresentable!

Cuirass is a strong and relatively light metal armor that protects the back and chest. The set includes leggings, bracers and a long leather jacket. Sometimes complemented by a legguard and a helmet.

Scaly is one of the oldest types of armor, imitating the scales of dragons and other powerful monsters. Metal scales are sewn onto a cloth or leather base, rattle menacingly and sparkle piercingly in the sun, informing others about the approach of the owner. The armor is easy to manufacture, although it has an impressive weight.
