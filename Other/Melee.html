Melee Weapones

Sai - a sharp blade similar to the tip of a spear. Sharpened “ears” extend from the base of the blade, which are used for disarming and slashing.

Flail - is a shock weight, a beater, attached by a chain to a short handle. Cheap and effective, although it requires some practice to use, especially when repelling enemy attacks. There are flails without a handle, they are called “extinguishing”.

The war hammer is a large two-handed hammer with a handle from 120 to 200 centimeters. As in the case of chasing, in addition to the hammer, the pommel is often equipped with an ax or beak.

Saber - the blade of a saber with a length of 80 to 110 centimeters has a characteristic bend towards the butt and, as a rule, one-sided sharpening. The saber in various modifications is widely used as the main melee weapon of cavalry and partly infantry.

Two-handed ax - a huge double-edged ax with a long handle! In an environment that tends towards realism, two-handed axes are rarely double-edged and just as rarely significantly larger than the heads of ordinary axes. The increase in impact force is achieved mainly due to the length of the handle.

Katana is a long curved sword with a one-sided edge. The handle allows for a two-handed grip. The length of the blade ranges from 60 to 75 centimeters.

A two-handed sword is a sword with a length of 120 to 180 centimeters. The longest and heaviest variants, the zweihänder, have a ricasso - the unsharpened part of the blade above the guard, designed for blocking. Also, it is often taken with the second hand to increase the force of the blow, so the ricasso is usually wrapped in leather and ends with a small guard, boar tusks.
